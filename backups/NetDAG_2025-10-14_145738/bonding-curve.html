Stability by Design: The NetDAG Bonding Curve

We’ll fill this page next with the overview, benefits, and a simple graph — step by step.

What is a Bonding Curve?

A Bonding Curve is a mathematical pricing model that ties a token’s price directly to its supply. Instead of order books, every buy or sell interacts with a smart contract that mints or burns tokens at the curve’s price.

As buys increase

More tokens are minted, supply rises, and the price moves up smoothly along a predictable curve.

As sells occur

Tokens are burned, supply falls, and the price moves down the same curve—gradually, not catastrophically.

Think of it like a self-balancing scale: every buy adds a little weight to the reserve and tips price up; every sell removes weight, and price adjusts down—without collapse.
[+] Learn the Math Behind the Price

The price on the curve is defined by a function. For clarity, consider a simple linear form:

P(S) = a·S + b
  • P(S) — price when total supply is S
  • a — slope (how quickly price rises with each token)
  • b — base price when supply is zero

When you mint ΔS tokens (from S₁ to S₂), you pay the area under the curve:

Cost(ΔS) = ∫S₁S₂ P(S) dS

This ensures part of every payment remains locked as backing, so NDG is asset-backed from the first purchase.

The Benefits: A Market Governed by Math

  • Dump-Resistant: Large holders can’t nuke the market in one move—each sale steps price along the curve.
  • Volatility-Smoothed: Movements are predictable and formula-driven, not ruled by hype or fear.
  • Self-Liquid: The contract is the market maker—there’s always a buyer and a seller on-chain.
