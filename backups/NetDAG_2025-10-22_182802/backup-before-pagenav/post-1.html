    Why a Bonding Curve? Stabilizing Price With Math

    Price is tied to supply. Liquidity is built-in. Volatility gets smoothed.

    Nov 2025 • Engineering

    Traditional token markets rely on order books where price is driven by emotion and thin liquidity. NetDAG uses a bonding curve so that price is a function of supply. Buys mint and add to a reserve, sells burn and release, and liquidity is always available from the contract.

    This design makes price movement smooth and predictable. The curve acts as a shock absorber: large sells step down the curve gradually rather than causing a collapse. It also makes pricing auditable and transparent to users.

    In NetDAG, the Guardian monitors volume and proposes parameter updates (e.g., slope or reserve ratio). Governance approves changes, so the economy remains transparent and adaptive.
