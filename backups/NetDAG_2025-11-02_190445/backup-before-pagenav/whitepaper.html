    NetDAG Whitepaper

    A decentralized framework for trust, stability, and real-world utility.

    Abstract

    NetDAG is a trust-first Web3 stack built on three pillars: a high-throughput Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG), a mathematically governed Bonding Curve that acts as a price shock absorber, and the NetDAG Guardian—an AI stability layer that analyzes network conditions and proposes parameter updates with governance oversight. Together they create predictable liquidity, low-latency settlement, and a permissionless platform for real-world utility such as Provenance and dVPN.

    Mission: Replace speculative fragility with verifiable, human-aligned utility. Build a crypto economy people can trust.

    Vision & Principles

    The NetDAG vision is a secure, intelligent, and human-aligned digital economy. Our design principles are: (1) Transparency by default—rules live in code; (2) Predictability—price/liquidity shaped by math; (3) Utility before speculation—products that solve real problems; (4) Governance in the open—changes proposed by the Guardian and ratified by community controls.

    System Architecture

    1) DAG Consensus

    The DAG structure enables concurrent validation rather than strict sequential blocks. As activity rises, throughput scales and confirmation latency remains low—ideal for micropayments, telemetry, and high-IO dApps.

    2) Adaptive Bonding Curve

    Price is a function of supply, e.g. P(S) = a·S + b. Every buy mints new tokens and locks the quote asset in a reserve; every sell burns tokens and releases from the reserve. The curve smooths volatility and ensures protocol-native liquidity without an order book.

    3) NetDAG Guardian (AI)

    The Guardian consumes on-chain telemetry (volume, buy/sell pressure, app usage, node quality), forecasts liquidity needs, detects anomalies, and proposes parameter updates (slope a, reserve ratio, fees). Proposals are gated by multisig/DAO—automation with human oversight.

    Economics & Token Model

    Supply & Allocations

    Max supply: 1,000,000,000 NDG (1B). Illustrative allocation (subject to final governance ratification):

    • Bonding Curve Reserve: 22% (liquidity floor and stability)
    • Ecosystem & Incentives (users, nodes, ambassadors): 30%
    • Community & Treasury (grants, growth): 18%
    • Presale/Public: 23% (starts at $0.03; staged increases)
    • Team: 7% (vesting and cliff safeguards)

    Curve Mechanics

    Buyers pay the area under the curve between supply states; a portion remains locked as reserve backing. Advanced safeguards include presale buy-only phases, vesting on large sells, and dynamic slope scheduling across growth epochs.

    Fees & Charity

    A protocol fee funds treasury, long-term reserves, and a charity pool (e.g., 2% of network revenue streams), aligning growth with impact. Disbursements are on-chain and community-auditable.

    Reference Applications

    NetDAG Provenance

    A QR-based authenticity layer: producers register batches; logistics append events; consumers scan to verify on-chain proof. Each scan contributes marginal liquidity via the bonding curve and a small share to the charity pool.

    NetDAG dVPN

    A peer-to-peer privacy network with automatic route selection by the Guardian. Node operators stake, earn NDG, and are scored by uptime/latency. Sessions settle quickly on the DAG; pricing remains predictable via the curve.

    Governance

    Changes to economic parameters and critical modules flow through proposal → review → on-chain approval. Early phases use a community-controlled multisig; long-term control transitions to a DAO. Guardian proposals are advisory until ratified.

    Security & Risk

    • Independent audits for core contracts and Guardian interfaces
    • Formal verification targets for curve math and reserve accounting
    • Rate limits & anomaly flags for extreme flow conditions
    • Transparent incident response and public post-mortems

    Roadmap (High-Level)

    • Q4 2025: Presale from $0.03; Guardian v1 monitoring; Provenance pilot
    • H1 2026: dVPN beta; Guardian proposals → multisig; first charities onboarded
    • H2 2026: Mainnet growth; DAO bootstrap; ecosystem grants
    • 2027–2029: Enterprise integrations, mobile SDKs, multi-region partnerships

    Conclusion

    NetDAG aligns performance, economics, and governance to build a durable, human-centric crypto economy. With a scalable DAG, a shock-absorbing bonding curve, and the predictive Guardian, NetDAG turns volatility into usable, transparent infrastructure for the next wave of Web3 adoption.
